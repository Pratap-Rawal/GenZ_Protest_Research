# Emotional Framing in Social Media vs Traditional Media: Evidence from Nepal’s Gen Z Protests

## Introduction

The rise of social media has transformed how political events are communicated and interpreted. Unlike traditional media, which follows structured reporting norms, social media allows individuals to express immediate reactions, often characterized by strong emotions.

This study examines whether social media framing is more emotional than traditional media coverage in the context of the 2025 Gen Z protests in Nepal. By comparing news articles with social media posts, the study seeks to understand differences in tone, framing, and emotional intensity.

## Conceptual Framework

This study builds on two key concepts: media framing and emotional expression.

Media framing refers to the way information is presented, influencing how audiences interpret events. Emotional expression refers to the intensity and direction of feelings conveyed in communication.

Traditional media is expected to maintain neutrality and structured reporting, while social media is likely to exhibit higher emotional intensity due to its informal and participatory nature.

## Methodology

### Data Sources

The study uses two datasets:
- News articles from Nepali media outlets
- Social media posts from platform X (Twitter)

### Sample

- 7 news articles
- 5 social media posts

### Variables

Each unit was coded based on:
- Tone (Neutral, Negative, Positive)
- Frame (Law and Order, State Repression, Democratic Rights)
- Emotion Level (Low, Medium, High)

### Analysis

Descriptive statistics were used to compare tone and emotion levels across the two datasets.

## Findings

### Tone Comparison

Traditional media coverage is predominantly neutral, with most articles maintaining a factual tone. In contrast, social media posts are largely negative, often expressing criticism, anger, or urgency.

### Emotion Levels

A clear difference is observed in emotional intensity. Social media posts are mostly high in emotional expression, while traditional media exhibits low emotional intensity.

### Framing Differences

While both media types use similar frames, social media posts tend to express these frames in a more direct and emotionally charged manner. Traditional media presents the same themes in a structured and controlled way.

## Discussion

The findings suggest that social media amplifies emotional responses to political events. This can be attributed to the participatory nature of social media, where individuals express personal reactions rather than structured reporting.

In contrast, traditional media follows professional norms that prioritize neutrality and factual reporting. As a result, differences between the two are not only in content but also in style and emotional intensity.

## Limitations

The study is based on a small sample size and focuses on a single event. Additionally, the coding of emotional intensity involves subjective interpretation.

Future research could expand the dataset and include multiple platforms for a more comprehensive analysis.

## Conclusion

This study finds that social media framing is significantly more emotional than traditional media coverage. While traditional media maintains neutrality, social media is characterized by high emotional intensity and direct expression.

These differences highlight the evolving nature of communication in the digital age and the importance of critically engaging with both forms of media.
