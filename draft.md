### Media Framing of the 2025 Gen Z Protests in Nepal: A Comparative Content Analysis of National and International News Coverage

*Media Framing of the 2025 Gen Z Protests in Nepal*


## Introduction
### Background  
The 2025 Gen Z protests in Nepal represent a significant moment of youth-led political mobilization. Triggered by dissatisfaction with governance, perceived nepotism, and restrictions on digital freedoms, these protests quickly gained national and international attention.

However, public understanding of such events is not shaped solely by what happens on the ground, but also by how events are presented in the media. Different media outlets may portray the same protest as a law-and-order issue, a democratic movement, or a case of state repression.

This study examines how various media platforms framed the protests and the use of police force, and how these frames may influence public perception.

### Research Problem

While the protests themselves have been widely discussed, there is limited systematic analysis of:

How Nepali vs international media framed the protests
Whether media emphasized protestor violence or state violence
How narratives differed across outlets

This study addresses this gap by conducting a comparative media framing analysis.

### Research Objectives
- To identify dominant frames used in reporting the protests
- To compare framing between national and international media
- To analyze how police action and protestor behavior are portrayed
- To assess whether media narratives differ in tone and emphasis

### 4. Research Questions
RQ1: What are the dominant frames used in media coverage of the protests?  
RQ2: How do Nepali media and international media differ in framing?  
RQ3: Do media reports emphasize protestor violence or state repression?  
RQ4: What tone (positive, negative, neutral) dominates coverage?

## Key Findings

The analysis of media coverage reveals a clear shift in framing as events unfolded. Initial reporting focused on protest escalation and law-and-order concerns, emphasizing the actions of protestors. However, following the occurrence of fatalities, the framing shifted toward state repression, with increasing emphasis on police violence and accountability.

As the situation intensified, the focus moved away from protestor actions toward the role of the state, particularly in terms of use of force. Later reporting further deepened this narrative by incorporating forensic evidence, indicating a transition from descriptive reporting to investigative and accountability-oriented journalism.
