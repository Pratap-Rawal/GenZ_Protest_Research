#### Thinking:

Q1: “Why did the same newspaper frame two events differently?”

It’s usually not about “manipulation” directly, but about what part of reality is emphasized.
1. DIFFERENT STAGES OF THE SAME EVENT (VERY IMPORTANT)
Article 1 → early phase
Focus: protest escalation, barricades, clashes
Frame: Law & Order
Article 2 → later phase
Focus: deaths, casualties
Frame: State Repression

👉 So:

The event changed, so the frame changed

🔹 2. NEWS VALUES (WHAT IS “NEWSWORTHY”)

Journalists select what is most important:

Conflict
Damage
Death
Urgency

👉 When deaths occur:
→ Coverage shifts naturally toward human impact
3. FRAMING ≠ LYING

Very important concept:

👉 Media is not necessarily distorting facts
👉 It is selecting which aspect to highlight

Example:

Same event can be framed as:

“Protesters storm parliament”
“Police kill protesters”

👉 Both can be true, but lead to different perceptions
🔹 4. YOUR POINT (REFINED)

Your idea about emotional impact can be improved like this:

Media coverage tends to emphasize the most impactful and newsworthy aspects of an event, such as violence or casualties, which may also evoke stronger emotional responses among audiences.

The same newspaper framed the two events differently because the nature of the events evolved over time. The first article focused on protest escalation and clashes, leading to a law-and-order frame. The second article reported fatalities, which shifted the focus toward state violence and resulted in a state repression frame. This suggests that media framing is influenced not only by editorial choices but also by the most newsworthy aspects of an event, particularly those involving human impact and casualties.

Framing is dynamic, not fixed
